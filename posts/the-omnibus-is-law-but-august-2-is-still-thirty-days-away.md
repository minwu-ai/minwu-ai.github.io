## The Legislative Process Is Complete. The Compliance Risk Is Not.

Four days ago, the EU Council gave its final approval to the AI Act simplification package — the Digital Omnibus on AI — following the European Parliament's formal endorsement on June 16, 2026. The co-legislative process is now complete. But "complete" and "in force" are not the same thing. The act will be published in the EU Official Journal shortly and enters into force on the third day after publication. Until then, the new dates are not binding and the August 2 threshold formally stands.

That gap — almost certainly days, not weeks — is where enterprise governance teams cannot blink.

## What the Omnibus Actually Changes

The architecture of the AI Act is unchanged. The deferral of high-risk applicability dates reflects a pragmatic acknowledgment that the regulatory infrastructure has not materialized on schedule — but it is a deferral, not a dismantling.

The headline changes, per [Gibson Dunn](https://www.gibsondunn.com/eu-ai-act-omnibus-agreement-postponed-high-risk-deadlines-and-other-key-changes/), [White & Case](https://www.whitecase.com/insight-alert/eu-agrees-digital-omnibus-deal-simplify-ai-rules), and the [EU Council press release](https://www.consilium.europa.eu/en/press/press-releases/2026/05/07/artificial-intelligence-council-and-parliament-agree-to-simplify-and-streamline-rules/):

| Obligation | Original Deadline | Omnibus Deadline |
|---|---|---|
| Annex III standalone high-risk systems | 2 August 2026 | **2 December 2027** |
| Annex I embedded high-risk systems | 2 August 2027 | **2 August 2028** |
| Article 50 transparency (new systems) | 2 August 2026 | **2 August 2026 — unchanged** |
| Article 50(2) watermarking (legacy systems) | 2 August 2026 | **2 December 2026** |
| Nudifier / NCII prohibition | N/A (new) | **2 December 2026** |

## August 2 Remains the Hard Line

> "2 August 2026 remains an active compliance date. The Article 50 transparency obligations are largely unaffected by the Omnibus. Businesses who are subject to those obligations should continue preparing for that date." — [Gibson Dunn](https://www.gibsondunn.com/eu-ai-act-omnibus-agreement-postponed-high-risk-deadlines-and-other-key-changes/)

August 2 is the date the Commission gains penalty enforcement powers over GPAI providers, Article 50 transparency obligations activate, and national market surveillance authorities can fully investigate and sanction breaches.

The practical distinction governance teams must operationalize immediately: AI systems already on the market before August 2 benefit from an extended watermarking deadline of December 2, 2026 — but all other systems face August 2 as written. Mapping which systems are already deployed, and when, is therefore a prerequisite for knowing which deadline actually applies.

This sequencing work was covered in [Europe's AI Labelling Clock Is Ticking](https://minwu-ai.github.io/europe-s-ai-labelling-clock-is-ticking-what-the-final-content-marking-code-means-for-governance-teams/) and the [prior Omnibus analysis](https://minwu-ai.github.io/council-vote-this-week-closes-the-legislative-loop-august-2-/). What is new is that the legislative process is fully closed — removing residual political uncertainty while sharpening the publication-timing risk into a 30-day sprint.

## The Nudifier Prohibition: Substantive Expansion, Not Just Calendar Relief

The Omnibus introduces a new prohibition on AI-generated non-consensual intimate imagery and child sexual abuse material into Article 5. For providers, the prohibition extends beyond systems *intended* for such use to any system where such generation is a reasonably foreseeable and reproducible outcome without significant technical modification, and the system lacks adequate safeguards to reliably prevent it. Providers of general-purpose image- or video-generation tools must therefore actively assess foreseeable misuse risks at design and deployment.

Violations carry penalties of up to €35 million or 7% of global annual turnover — the highest ceiling in the entire Act. Any provider of multimodal or video-synthesis tooling should treat December 2, 2026 as a hard product-risk deadline.

## Historical Parallel: GDPR's Implementation Gap

The closest precedent is GDPR's 2018 enforcement date. Harmonized standards and national authority capacity lagged; enforcement was geographically uneven for years. The AI Act has tried to learn from that by fixing conditional backstop dates — but those dates apply regardless of whether harmonized standards are ready. If standards arrive late, the dates don't move with them. The Omnibus pushed that structural risk forward; it did not resolve it.
